for work use, 3DoF is useful? (seeing both glass, and see-through to macbook screen)

2025-07-11 by gemini ai​

TLDR: 3DoF is needed

Without 3DoF (transparent lenses): It would be perpetually "stuck" in the center of your vision. it's not a practical or comfortable way to use a virtual screen alongside a physical one, because the virtual screen keeps getting in the way or shifting whenever you move your head even slightly.

The VITURE Pro glasses offer a compelling solution for productivity, especially when it comes to having a larger, portable display. Let's break down how 3DoF (3 Degrees of Freedom) factors in for work use, particularly with seeing both the virtual screen and your MacBook screen:

Understanding 3DoF with VITURE Pro:

  • What 3DoF means: 3DoF refers to tracking rotational movements (pitch, yaw, and roll – tilting your head up/down, left/right, and side-to-side). The virtual screen will remain fixed in space relative to your head's rotation. This means if you turn your head, the screen will appear to stay in the same "spot" in your perceived environment.
  • SpaceWalker App for Mac: VITURE's SpaceWalker app for Mac is crucial here. It allows you to project multiple virtual screens from your MacBook onto your VITURE Pro glasses. By default, these virtual screens are "locked in space" using 3DoF. You can set up various layouts, including multiple side-by-side displays or an ultrawide panoramic display.
  • "See-through" to MacBook screen: The VITURE Pro glasses have electrochromic lenses that can tint, allowing you to control the level of transparency. This means you can have the virtual screens in front of you while still being able to see your physical MacBook screen (and your surroundings) through the lenses. The film can even automatically turn off when you look down, making it easier to see your keyboard.

Is 3DoF useful for work use (seeing both glass and MacBook screen)?

Yes, 3DoF is generally useful for this work scenario, with some nuances:

  • Virtual Monitor Setup: 3DoF allows the virtual displays to stay put in your field of view as you naturally move your head. This is essential for a multi-monitor setup where you'd be looking between different virtual screens, much like you would with physical monitors.
  • Productivity Boost: Reviews indicate that the VITURE Pro, especially with the SpaceWalker app, can significantly boost productivity by providing a large, multi-screen workspace even when you're on the go. Users report it's like having a full multi-screen experience on a plane or in a hotel room.
  • Seamless Integration: The glasses connect via USB-C and your MacBook treats them as an external display, making setup straightforward.
  • See-through Capability: The ability to adjust the electrochromic lenses is a key feature. It allows you to transition between an immersive virtual environment and seeing your physical surroundings (including your MacBook's keyboard and screen) without taking the glasses off. This is very practical for typing, interacting with your laptop, or simply staying aware of your environment.

Potential Considerations (related to 3DoF and general use):

  • Drift: Some users have reported minor "drift" where the virtual screens might subtly shift over time, requiring occasional recentering. However, this is generally considered manageable with quick re-calibration shortcuts.
  • 6DoF vs. 3DoF: It's important to note that 3DoF only tracks rotation, not translation (moving forward, backward, up, down). For stationary work at a desk, 3DoF is largely sufficient as you're primarily moving your head to look at different parts of the virtual display. If you needed to physically move around a virtual environment, you'd need 6DoF (which some higher-end XR devices offer, though often at a higher price point and with a bulkier form factor). The VITURE Luma series is noted to offer 6DoF within the SpaceWalker app for all models.
  • Resolution and Text Clarity: While the VITURE Pro offers 1080p resolution per eye and a sharp image, some users accustomed to high-end physical monitors might find the text clarity not quite on par for very small fonts, though it's generally considered good enough for most productivity tasks.
  • Field of View (FOV): With a 46-degree FOV, you will see the edges of the display. This is common for display glasses and generally not a significant issue for productivity, as we're used to seeing the edges of physical monitors.
  • Smooth Follow: The SpaceWalker app has a "Smooth Follow" feature where the display moves with your head, but this disables 3DoF. It's generally recommended for single displays, not multi-display layouts where fixed screens are preferred.

In conclusion, for your stated work use case of having a larger virtual display while still being able to see your MacBook screen, the VITURE Pro with its 3DoF capabilities and the SpaceWalker app is a highly useful tool. The ability to create a multi-monitor setup and the see-through functionality make it a strong contender for portable productivity.
